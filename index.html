    My Experience

    Laboratory Experience

    Constructing a Copper Biosensor using Genetic Engineering

    The lab aimed to engineer E. coli for copper ion detection by inserting the copper-sensitive pCusC promoter into an asPink-promoterless plasmid, enabling asPink protein expression in response to copper levels. PCR amplified the pCusC promoter, followed by plasmid miniprep, gel electrophoresis, molecular cloning via restriction digestion and purification, and ligation, culminating in E. coli transformation. Screening on antibiotic and copper-containing agar, and validation through restriction enzyme fragment patterns and gel electrophoresis, assessed cloning success. Spectrophotometry and SDS-PAGE analyzed asPink protein levels, verifying the biosensor's functionality by correlating copper concentration with protein expression.
